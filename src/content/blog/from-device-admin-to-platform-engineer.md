## The university years

Managing thousands of Macs at a university taught me the fundamentals: imaging, MDM, scripting, and the art of keeping faculty and students productive. This section will cover the day-to-day realities, the tools I used (Jamf, Munki, custom scripts), and how I started thinking beyond "fix the machine" toward "fix the system."

## Open source as a gateway

Contributing to open source projects—particularly those used in Mac fleet management—gave me visibility into how real software is built. I'll discuss specific projects, what I learned about code quality and collaboration, and how that experience made the transition to engineering feel less like a leap and more like a natural next step.

## Making the leap to enterprise

Moving from higher ed to a tech company meant a different scale, different stakeholders, and different expectations. This section will cover the interview process, how I framed my IT experience as engineering-relevant, and the first few months of proving I could deliver product-quality work.

## When IT becomes engineering

The mindset shift: from reactive support to proactive platform building. Topics include treating internal tools as products, writing tests and documentation, and building for maintainability rather than just "getting it done."

## Advice for others making the shift

Concrete tips for device admins, sysadmins, and IT professionals who want to move into platform engineering or software development. What to learn, how to build a portfolio, and how to tell your story in interviews.
